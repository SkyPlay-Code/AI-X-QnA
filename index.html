Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (AI)

1. What is intelligence?

Answer: Intelligence is the ‘ability to perceive or infer information, and to retain it as knowledge to be applied towards adaptive behaviours within an environment or context’.


2. What abilities are involved in intelligence?

Answer: The abilities that are involved in intelligence are:

  1. Mathematical Logical Reasoning: A person's ability to regulate, measure, and understand numerical symbols, abstraction, and logic.

  2. Linguistic Intelligence: Language processing skills both in terms of understanding or implementation in writing or verbally.

  3. Spatial Visual Intelligence: The ability to perceive the visual world and the relationship of one object to another.

  4. Kinaesthetic Intelligence: Ability that is related to how a person uses his limbs in a skilled manner.

  5. Musical Intelligence: A person's ability to recognize and create sounds, rhythms, and sound patterns.

  6. Intrapersonal Intelligence: Describes how high the level of self-awareness someone has is, starting from realizing weakness, strength, to his own feelings.

  7. Existential Intelligence: An additional category of intelligence relating to religious and spiritual awareness.

  8. Naturalist Intelligence: An additional category of intelligence relating to the ability to process information on the environment around us.

  9. Interpersonal Intelligence: The ability to communicate with others by understanding other people's feelings & influence of the person.



3. What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?

Answer: When a machine possesses the ability to mimic human traits, i.e., make decisions, predict the future, learn and improve on its own, it is said to have artificial intelligence. In other words, a machine is artificially intelligent when it can accomplish tasks by itself - collect data, understand it, analyse it, learn from it, and improve it.


4. How do machines become artificially intelligent?

Answer: Machines become intelligent once they are trained with some information which helps them achieve their tasks. AI machines also keep updating their knowledge to optimize their output.


5. Give some examples of AI applications.

Answer: Examples of AI applications include apps that monitor physical and mental health, humanoids like Sophia, biometric security systems, real-time language translators, and weather forecasts.


6. What differentiates automation from AI?

Answer: Any machine that has been trained with data and can make decisions/predictions on its own can be termed as AI. A fully automatic washing machine requires human intervention to select the parameters of washing and to do the necessary preparation for it to function correctly before each wash, which makes it an example of automation, not AI. 7. What is the relationship between AI, Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL)?

Answer:

Definition:

  • AI: Any technique enabling computers to mimic human intelligence.

  • ML: A subset of AI that enables machines to improve at tasks with experience (data).

  • DL: Enables software to train itself to perform tasks with vast amounts of data. Machines are intelligent enough to develop algorithms for themselves.

Capabilities:

  • AI: Machines can recognize faces, manipulate objects, and understand voice commands.

  • ML: Machines learn by themselves using provided data to make accurate predictions/decisions.

  • DL: Machines train themselves with huge amounts of data and can develop algorithms independently.

Intelligence Level:

  • AI: Covers all concepts and algorithms that mimic human intelligence.

  • ML: Intermediately intelligent.

  • DL: The most advanced form of AI.

Data Requirement:

  • AI: Training data is needed.

  • ML: Requires data to learn and make predictions.

  • DL: Requires vast amounts of data for training.

Relationship: ML and DL are subsets of AI, but not all Machine Learning is Deep Learning.

Algorithms:

  • AI: Thinks algorithmically and executes what they have been asked for intelligently.

  • DL: Intelligent enough to develop algorithms for themselves.


8. What are the three main domains of AI based on the type of data used?

Answer: The three domains are:

  1. Data Science: This domain deals with data systems and processes. It involves collecting numerous data, maintaining data sets, and deriving meaning from them to make decisions.

  2. Computer Vision (CV): This domain enables machines to get and analyze visual information, predicting decisions based on it. The process includes image acquisition, screening, analysis, and information extraction, helping computers understand visual content. Examples include self-driving cars and facial recognition.

  3. Natural Language Processing (NLP): This branch focuses on the interaction between computers and humans using natural language. NLP aims to enable computers to understand and process human languages. Examples include email filters and smart assistants.



9. What are the ethical concerns in AI?

Answer: Ethical concerns in AI includes moral issues:

  • AI Ethics: As AI usage grows, it's crucial to consider ethical practices in AI solution development.

  • Moral Issues: AI developers face moral dilemmas when designing algorithms, as their own moralities can influence machine decision-making.

  • Data Privacy: AI relies heavily on data, raising concerns about how companies collect and use personal data. Users often unknowingly grant apps access to their data.

  • AI Bias: Biases can be unintentionally transferred from developers to AI systems, leading to skewed outcomes. For example, virtual assistants often have female voices. Search results can also reflect biases.

  • AI Access: Unequal access to AI technology can create a gap between those who can afford it and those who cannot.

  • Job Displacement: AI may replace human labor, potentially causing mass unemployment for those in laborious jobs without specialized skills.

  • Over-Reliance on AI: Over-dependence on AI, especially in education, can hinder the development of critical thinking skills in children.

7. How does the 4Ws Problem Canvas help in Problem Scoping?

Answer: The 4Ws Problem Canvas helps in identifying the key elements related to the problem. It helps to have a deeper understanding around a problem so that the picture becomes clearer while working to solve it. The 4Ws stand for:

  1. Who: This block helps in analyzing the people getting affected directly or indirectly due to the problem. It identifies the stakeholders and what is known about them. Stakeholders are the people who face this problem and would be benefitted with the solution.

  2. What: This block determines the nature of the problem. It identifies what the problem is and how you know that it is a problem. It also involves gathering evidence to prove that the problem you have selected actually exists.

  3. Where: This block focuses on the context/situation/location of the problem. It helps look into the situation in which the problem arises, the context of it, and the locations where it is prominent.

  4. Why: This block focuses on the benefits which the stakeholders would get from the solution and how it will benefit them as well as the society. It helps to understand why you want to solve this problem.

19. Discuss the importance of the Evaluation stage and explain key evaluation metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score.

Answer: Once a model has been made and trained, it needs to go through proper testing so that one can calculate the efficiency and performance of the model.

Key evaluation metrics include:

  • Accuracy: Defined as the percentage of correct predictions out of all the observations. A prediction can be said to be correct if it matches the reality.

  • Precision: Defined as the percentage of true positive cases versus all the cases where the prediction is true. That is, it takes into account the True Positives and False Positives.

  • Recall: Defined as the fraction of positive cases that are correctly identified. It majorly takes into account the true reality cases where in Reality there was a fire, but the machine either detected it correctly or it didn’t. That is, it considers True Positives and False Negatives.

  • F1 Score: Defined as the measure of balance between precision and recall.

24. What is unsupervised learning?
Answer: Unsupervised learning is a type of AI modeling where a machine learns by itself. In this approach, the AI model is trained using data and can then design a model that adapts to changes in the data. The model adjusts itself based on changes in the data to handle exceptions.

Key aspects of unsupervised learning:

  • Unlabelled dataset Unsupervised learning models work on unlabelled datasets. This means the data fed to the machine is random, and the trainer might not have information about it.

  • Identifying patterns These models identify relationships, patterns, and trends in the data. They help users understand the data and the major features identified by the machine.

  • Example For example, a user might have random data consisting of 1000 dog images and want to understand patterns in it. The user would feed this data into the unsupervised learning model and train the machine on it. After training, the machine identifies patterns, such as color or size of the dogs.

  • Clustering Unsupervised learning algorithms can cluster unknown data based on identified patterns or trends. The patterns observed might be known to the developer or new.

  • Dimensionality Reduction Datasets can have many dimensions, and with each added dimension, information can be lost. Dimensionality reduction is used to reduce the dimensions while still retaining the ability to make sense of the data.


25. What is neural network?

Answer:

  • Neural networks are modelled after neurons in the human brain. Their key advantage is the ability to automatically extract data features without needing programmer input.

  • A neural network is a system that organizes machine learning algorithms to perform tasks. It provides a fast and efficient way to solve problems with very large datasets, such as images.

  • Neural networks generally perform better with larger amounts of data, whereas traditional machine learning algorithms stop improving after a certain saturation point.

  • A neural network is divided into multiple layers, each containing blocks called nodes. Each node has its own task, which it passes to the next layer.

  • The layers of a neural network include:

  • Input layer: Acquires data and feeds it to the neural network. No processing occurs in this layer.

  • Hidden layers: These layers perform the processing. Each node in the hidden layers executes its machine learning algorithm on the data it gets from the input layer. The processed output is then fed to the next hidden layer. A neural network system can have multiple hidden layers, depending on the complexity required.

  • Output layer: This layer acquires the final processed data from the last hidden layer and presents it to the user as the final output. The output layer does not process the data; it is meant for user interface.

  • The KNN algorithm assumes that similar things exist in close proximity.

  • The KNN prediction model relies on the surrounding points or neighbors to determine its class or group. It utilizes the properties of the majority of the nearest points to decide how to classify unknown points, based on the concept that similar data points should be close to each other.

Answer: The Computer Vision domain of Artificial Intelligence, enables machines to see through images or visual data, process and analyse them on the basis of algorithms and methods in order to analyse actual phenomena with images.

5. Define "pixel" and explain its basic function in digital images.

Answer: A "pixel" is short for "picture element." It is the smallest individual unit of information in a digital image.

Basic Function:

  • Pixels are the fundamental building blocks of digital images. They are arranged in a grid-like pattern, and each pixel contains color and brightness information.  

  • By combining the color and brightness values of numerous pixels, a digital image is formed.

  • Essentially, they are the little dots that when combined, create a digital picture.

The more of these dots there are, the more detailed the picture will be.  


6. Define "resolution" in the context of digital images and explain how it relates to pixel count.

Answer:

Definition:

  • In the context of digital images, "resolution" refers to the level of detail or sharpness of an image.

  • It directly correlates with the number of pixels that make up the image.

  • Higher resolution generally means more pixels, resulting in a sharper and more detailed image.

Relationship to Pixel Count:

  • Resolution is essentially determined by the total number of pixels present in an image.

  • More pixels allow for finer details to be captured and displayed.

7. Describe the two common conventions for expressing image resolution.

Answer:

Common Conventions for Expressing Image Resolution:

  • Width x Height:

    • This method expresses resolution as the number of pixels along the width of the image multiplied by the number of pixels along the height.

    • Example: A 1920x1080 image has 1920 pixels horizontally and 1080 pixels vertically.

  • Megapixels:

    • This method expresses the total number of pixels in an image in millions.

    • Example: A 12-megapixel camera can capture images with approximately 12 million pixels.



8. Define "pixel value" and explain its basic function in digital images.

Answer: "Pixel value" describes the brightness and/or color of a pixel. Its function is to define the visual appearance of each individual point in a digital image.



9. Explain how pixel brightness and/or color are represented using pixel values. Describe the common format of a byte image and its value range.

Answer: Pixel values, stored as 8-bit integers in a byte image, dictate brightness/color. The byte image format has values from 0 (black/no color) to 255 (white/full color), with intermediate values representing shades of gray or varying colors.



10. Describe the relationship between bits, bytes, and the range of pixel values in a byte image. Explain why a byte image has 256 (0-255) possible values.

Answer: A byte is 8 bits. Each bit has 2 states (0 or 1). Therefore, 2^8 = 256 possible combinations, resulting in pixel values from 0 to 255.



11. What is a greyscale image, and what are the pixel values representing black and white?

Answer: A greyscale image consists of shades of gray without apparent color. Black is represented by a pixel value of 0, and white by 255.





Natural Language Processing

1. What are the applications of NLP?

Answer:

  • Virtual Assistants: Systems like Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant use NLP to understand spoken commands, provide information, and perform tasks.

  • Search Engines: Search engines like Google use NLP to understand the intent behind search queries and provide more relevant results.
  • Predictive Text: Features like autocorrect and autocomplete on smartphones use NLP to anticipate and suggest words as you type.
  • Email Filtering: NLP helps to filter out spam and to categorize emails into different catagories.
